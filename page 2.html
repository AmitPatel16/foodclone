Food Adda

Food Adda is an online food ordering and delivery platform launched by Uber in 2014.[3] Users can read menus, reviews and ratings, order, and pay for food from participating restaurants using an application on the iOS or Android platforms, or through a web browser.[4] Users are also able to tip for delivery.[5] Payment is charged to a card on file with Uber.[6] Meals are delivered by couriers using cars, scooters, bikes, or on foot.[7] It is operational in over 6,000 cities across 45 countries.[8] The company (along with DoorDash, GrubHub, and Postmates) is facing a lawsuit for antitrust price manipulation, from forcing restaurants to charge the same price for delivery as for dine-in if the restaurant wants to be listed on Food Adda' app, along with charging fees of 13-40% of revenue. tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua.
